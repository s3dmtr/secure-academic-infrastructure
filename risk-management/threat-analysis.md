# 🚨 Threat Analysis

## Overview

Educational institutions are exposed to multiple cybersecurity threats due to their dependence on digital services, large user populations, and continuous access to online systems.

XYZ Academy applies threat analysis practices to identify potential attacks that may affect organizational systems, academic operations, and sensitive information assets.

---

## Threat Landscape

The project environment includes several realistic cybersecurity threats commonly targeting academic institutions.

### Common Threats

- Phishing attacks
- Ransomware infections
- Malware outbreaks
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Credential theft
- Insider misuse
- Weak password exploitation
- Service disruptions

These threats may impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of institutional systems and data.

---

## Threat Categories

| Threat Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Social Engineering | Manipulating users to reveal sensitive information |
| Malware | Malicious software targeting systems or data |
| Unauthorized Access | Access attempts without proper authorization |
| Insider Threats | Misuse of privileges by authorized users |
| Service Disruptions | Events affecting system availability |
| Credential Attacks | Attempts to compromise user authentication data |

---

## Phishing Threat Scenario

### Attack Description

An attacker sends a fake email pretending to be from the academy IT department requesting urgent account verification.

The email contains a malicious login page designed to capture user credentials.

### Potential Impact

- Compromised user accounts
- Unauthorized access to LMS services
- Exposure of academic records
- Internal phishing propagation

### Mitigation Measures

- User awareness training
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Email verification procedures
- Suspicious activity reporting

---

## Ransomware Threat Scenario

### Attack Description

A malicious attachment disguised as an academic document is opened on a staff device, triggering ransomware execution.

The malware attempts to encrypt local and shared files.

### Potential Impact

- Loss of academic services
- Restricted access to student records
- Operational downtime
- Recovery costs

### Mitigation Measures

- Offline backups
- Endpoint protection
- Software updates
- Restricted file-sharing permissions

---

## Insider Misuse

Authorized users with excessive privileges may intentionally or unintentionally expose sensitive information or misuse institutional systems.

### Examples

- Unauthorized sharing of academic records
- Improper use of administrative systems
- Weak password practices

### Mitigation Measures

- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Accountability monitoring
- Security awareness activities
- Access reviews

---

## Threat Analysis Importance

Threat analysis improves organizational awareness of cybersecurity risks and supports proactive security planning, operational readiness, and incident response preparation.
