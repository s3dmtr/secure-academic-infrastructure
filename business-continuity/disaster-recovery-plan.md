# 🛠️ Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

## Overview

XYZ Academy applies Disaster Recovery Planning (DRP) procedures to restore critical systems and services following cybersecurity incidents, infrastructure failures, or operational disruptions.

The recovery process focuses on restoring academic services efficiently while minimizing operational downtime and data loss.

---

## Recovery Objectives

The disaster recovery strategy aims to:

- Restore critical academic systems
- Minimize operational downtime
- Protect institutional data
- Support backup restoration
- Improve recovery coordination
- Maintain service availability

---

## Recovery Priorities

Critical systems are restored according to operational importance.

| System | Recovery Priority |
|---|---|
| Academic Records System | High |
| Learning Management System (LMS) | High |
| Staff Communication Services | High |
| Backup Systems | High |
| Administrative Systems | Medium |
| Wireless Network Services | Medium |

---

## Recovery Objectives (RTO & RPO)

The project environment applies recovery objectives to support operational continuity.

| Objective | Description |
|---|---|
| RTO (Recovery Time Objective) | Maximum acceptable downtime before services are restored |
| RPO (Recovery Point Objective) | Maximum acceptable amount of data loss during disruption |

### Recovery Targets

- RTO: 4–6 hours
- RPO: 24 hours

---

## Backup Strategy

The organization applies multiple backup methods to improve recovery readiness.

### Backup Methods

- Daily automated backups
- Weekly full-system backups
- Cloud-based backup storage
- Offline backup storage for critical data

Backup integrity should be verified regularly to ensure recovery reliability.

---

## Disaster Recovery Workflow

When a major disruption occurs:

1. The incident is identified and assessed.
2. Affected systems are isolated if necessary.
3. Backup restoration procedures are initiated.
4. Critical systems are restored based on recovery priorities.
5. Restored systems are tested and validated.
6. Users are informed about service restoration.
7. The incident is documented for future improvement.

---

## Recovery Validation

Recovered systems should be validated to ensure:

- System functionality
- Data integrity
- Service availability
- Secure user access

Validation activities help confirm successful recovery operations.

---

## Organizational Resilience

Effective disaster recovery planning improves organizational resilience by reducing downtime, supporting operational continuity, and improving preparedness during cybersecurity incidents and infrastructure failures.
